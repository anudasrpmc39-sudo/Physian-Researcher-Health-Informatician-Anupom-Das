    Professional Profile

    About Me

    Physician–researcher with more than eight years of experience in clinical medicine, epidemiology, disease surveillance, and health systems research.

    Professional background spans frontline clinical care during the COVID-19 pandemic, public health emergency response, government health services, and the implementation of large-scale clinical and community-based research studies.

    Currently pursuing a Joint Master’s in Health Informatics at Karolinska Institutet and Stockholm University as a recipient of the Swedish Institute Scholarship for Global Professionals (SISGP, 2025–2027).

    Training focuses on :

  • Health informatics
  • Digitalization of Health workflow
  • Data Science, Machine learning, and Artificial Intellegence

    • My primary research interest lies at the intersection of health informatics, epidemiology, and implementation science. I am particularly interested in using real-world health data, electronic health records, digital health systems, and advanced analytical methods to generate evidence that improves clinical decision-making, strengthens disease surveillance, and enhances health system performance.

    My long-term goal is to pursue PhD and postdoctoral research that combines rigorous epidemiological methods with health informatics and data science to address clinically relevant and policy-important questions. I aim to contribute to translational research that transforms routine healthcare data into actionable knowledge and advances equitable, efficient, and patient-centered healthcare systems globally.

    Research Portfolio

    My Current Projects

    Each project addresses a critical gap in public health knowledge with robust methodology and multi-stakeholder partnerships.

    01

    AI-Powered Disease Surveillance System for Low-Resource Settings

    ● Active

    This project develops and validates a machine learning-driven disease surveillance platform for health facilities with limited digital infrastructure. The system integrates real-time data from community health workers, facility reports, and environmental sensors to enable early outbreak detection with a minimum 5-day advance warning.

    📅 Duration: 2023 – 2026
    💰 Funder: Wellcome Trust / NIH
    🌍 Sites: 3 countries
    📄 Project Page 📊 Data Dashboard 📖 Publications
    02

    Non-Communicable Disease Prevention Through Community Engagement

    ● Ongoing

    A community-based participatory research project evaluating culturally-adapted health promotion interventions on NCD risk factors including hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. Uses a cluster-randomised controlled trial design across 24 communities with a 3-year follow-up.

    📅 Duration: 2022 – 2025
    💰 Funder: WHO / National Research Fund
    👥 Participants: 4,800
    📄 Protocol Paper 🗺️ Trial Registry
    03

    Electronic Health Records Implementation & Quality Improvement

    ● Ongoing

    This implementation science project evaluates barriers, facilitators, and health outcomes associated with EHR adoption in public district hospitals. Using a mixed-methods approach, the research develops a practical implementation framework for national scale-up with focus on interoperability and data quality.

    📅 Duration: 2024 – 2027
    💰 Funder: Digital Health Initiative
    🏥 Sites: 18 hospitals
    📄 Study Overview 🔧 Open Toolkit
    04

    Antimicrobial Resistance Epidemiology in Community Settings

    ⚙ Planning Phase

    A prospective cohort study investigating the emergence, spread, and determinants of antimicrobial resistance in community settings. Leverages genomic surveillance, spatial epidemiology, and social network analysis to map transmission pathways and identify high-risk population clusters.

    📅 Duration: 2025 – 2029
    💰 Funder: Under review – UKRI
    🔬 Approach: Genomic + Spatial
